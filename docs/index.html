An AI teammate sitting next to your tabs.

Anya lives in your browser sidebar. Ask it to summarise the page, fill the form, click through the wizard, dig across your tabs, or tidy your bookmarks. It sees what you see and uses your real, logged-in browser — nothing leaves your laptop.

Install — one line, PowerShell 7+

01 · What you can do

The things you'd ask a teammate
looking over your shoulder.

  1. 01

    Summarise anything you're reading.

    Long PR, RFC, JIRA ticket, news article? Type @tab tldr in 5 bullets. Anya pulls the page text and answers in the sidebar — no copy-paste, no second tab.

  2. 02

    Drive your browser, hands-free.

    "Fill this expense form with the receipt I'm looking at." "Click through the wizard and screenshot the result." Anya uses your logged-in browser, so no reauth, no captchas you have to redo.

  3. 03

    Dig across your open tabs.

    "Compare what I have open in GitHub with the spec in the other tab." @tabs gives Anya a manifest of everything open. Synthesise without alt-tabbing.

  4. 04

    Tidy your bookmarks.

    "Group these into AI / infra / fun." Anya proposes the plan, waits for your OK, then bulk-moves and renames. Find dead links and dupes the same way.

  5. 05

    Ambient context, never copy-paste.

    @selection, @clipboard, @url — first-class inputs. Highlight a paragraph, type "explain this like I'm five", get an answer. Stop screenshotting things into other chats.

  6. 06

    Fully local. Yours.

    Your conversations live in your browser. The bridge runs on your laptop. No telemetry. No cloud sync. No account to create. Use your existing Copilot subscription.

02 · How you talk to it

Three little prefixes.
That's the whole language.

Just type — nothing special needed. But when you want to be precise, three characters give Anya everything it needs.

@

context

"Look at what I'm looking at."

Pulls in pieces of your browser — automatically inlined before sending.

  • @tab   this page
  • @selection   what I've highlighted
  • @tabs   everything open
  • @clipboard   what I last copied
/

commands

"Do something to the sidebar."

Quick actions on your chat — never reaches the model.

  • /new   start a fresh chat
  • /pin · /tag add ux
  • /search ordering
  • /export   save as Markdown
#

references

"Look up the thing I labelled X."

Named lookups — bookmarks, prior chats, saved snippets.

  • #bookmark/…
  • #chat/…

03 · Install

One line. Any Chromium.

PowerShell 7+ on Windows, macOS, or Linux. Works in Edge, Chrome, Brave, Vivaldi, Arc, Chromium.

pwsh 
# clones to ~/Anya and sets everything up
> irm https://raw.githubusercontent.com/aasis21/Anya/main/install.ps1 | iex

› cloning aasis21/Anya → ~/Anya
› building bridge/  ✓
› building extension/  ✓
› bridge ping → PONG
› registered native-messaging host for:
   ✓ Microsoft Edge
   ✓ Google Chrome
   ✓ Brave

done. load ~/Anya/extension/dist in your browser.

Then, in your browser

  1. Open edge://extensions (or your browser's equivalent)
  2. Toggle Developer mode
  3. Load unpacked → pick ~/Anya/extension/dist/
  4. Pin the action icon → click → sidebar opens
  5. Type ping → expect PONG

Options

-InstallDir <path>where to clone (default ~/Anya)
-Branch <name>track a non-main branch
-Browsers edge,chromeregister only specific browsers

Want to hack on it?

# developing locally — clone manually
> git clone https://github.com/aasis21/Anya
> cd Anya; ./setup.ps1   # or ./setup.sh
04 · Under the hood

Two small pieces.
That's the whole thing.

A sidebar extension talks to a tiny Node bridge on your laptop. The bridge wraps the official @github/copilot-sdk and shells out to Playwright when Anya needs to drive a tab. No servers, no relays, no telemetry.

┌────────────────────┐    JSON frames    ┌────────────────────┐
│  Browser sidebar   │ ◀─────────────▶   │  Node bridge       │
│  (Lit + marked)    │  Native Messaging │  @github/copilot-  │
│                    │                   │  sdk + tools       │
└────────────────────┘                   └────────────────────┘
        │                                          │
        │ chrome.tabs / scripting                  │ shells out to
        ▼                                          ▼
